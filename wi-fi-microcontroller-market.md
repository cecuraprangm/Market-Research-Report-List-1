<p><p>Wi-Fi microcontrollers are divided into three main market types based on their frequency bands: Sub-1 GHz, 2.4 GHz, and other markets. Sub-1 GHz microcontrollers operate on frequencies below 1 GHz, offering longer range and better penetration through walls. 2.4 GHz microcontrollers are the most commonly used, providing faster data rates but shorter range. Other markets include microcontrollers operating on different frequency bands for specialized applications such as industrial or automotive use. Each market type has its own unique advantages and applications in the Wi-Fi microcontroller industry.</p></p>
