<p><p>Image Guided Radiotherapy (IGRT) is a technique used in radiation oncology that combines imaging technology with radiation therapy to precisely target cancerous cells and minimize damage to healthy tissues. It involves the use of advanced imaging tools such as CT scans, MRI scans, and X-rays to guide the delivery of radiation to the tumor site. By continually imaging the tumor before and during treatment, IGRT enables doctors to adjust the radiation delivery in real-time based on any changes in the tumor's position, shape, or size.</p><p>The Image Guided Radiotherapy Market is expected to witness significant growth during the forecast period. Factors driving the market growth include the increasing prevalence of cancer, advancements in imaging technology, rising adoption of IGRT in radiation oncology, and the growing demand for minimally invasive procedures. Moreover, the benefits of IGRT over conventional radiation therapy techniques, such as improved tumor targeting and reduced side effects, are also contributing to market growth.</p><p>Furthermore, the market is witnessing several trends that are shaping its growth trajectory. One of the major trends is the integration of IGRT with other treatment modalities such as intensity-modulated radiation therapy (IMRT) and proton therapy, which enhances the precision and effectiveness of cancer treatment. Additionally, the development of advanced imaging techniques, such as cone-beam computed tomography (CBCT) and magnetic resonance-guided radiotherapy (MRgRT), is further propelling the growth of the IGRT market.</p><p>In conclusion, the Image Guided Radiotherapy Market is projected to grow at a steady CAGR of 8.50% during the forecast period.
