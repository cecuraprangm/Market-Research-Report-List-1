<p><p>Laminated glass for building is primarily categorized into three types based on the interlayer material used: PVB (polyvinyl butyral), EVA (ethylene-vinyl acetate), and SGP (SentryGlas Plus). PVB is commonly used due to its cost-effectiveness and easy availability. EVA is known for its superior adhesion properties, making it ideal for structural applications. SGP offers high strength and toughness, making it suitable for hurricane-impact-resistant structures. There are also other niche markets that utilize alternative interlayer materials for specific requirements within the laminated glass for building industry.</p></p>
