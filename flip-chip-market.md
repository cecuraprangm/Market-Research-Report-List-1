<p><p>Flip-chip is a method of interconnecting semiconductor devices, such as microchips, with external circuitry by flipping them upside down and attaching the bond pads directly to the substrate. This allows for shorter interconnections, reduced parasitic effects, and better heat dissipation, resulting in higher performance and reliability.</p><p>The global flip-chip market is experiencing significant growth, driven by the increasing demand for advanced packaging technologies in devices such as smartphones, tablets, and other electronics. The adoption of flip-chip technology is also being driven by its ability to meet the requirements of high-performance applications in industries such as automotive, aerospace, and telecommunications. </p><p>The market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 11.3% during the forecast period, with key trends including the development of advanced flip-chip technologies such as Cu pillar and microbump technologies, the increasing adoption of flip-chip in high-speed applications, and the shift towards wafer-level packaging to reduce costs and improve efficiency. The demand for flip-chip technology is expected to continue to rise as manufacturers seek to enhance the performance and reliability of their products.</p></p>
