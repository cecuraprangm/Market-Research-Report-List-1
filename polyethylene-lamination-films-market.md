<p><p>Polyethylene lamination films are classified based on their thickness into four types: up to 0.1 mm, 0.1-0.5 mm, 0.5-1 mm, and above 1 mm. Films up to 0.1 mm are used for light packaging applications, while those in the 0.1-0.5 mm range are commonly used in food packaging. Films in the 0.5-1 mm category find applications in heavy-duty packaging, construction, and agriculture. Lamination films above 1 mm thickness are used for industrial purposes such as insulation and protective coatings. Each type caters to specific market needs and requirements.</p></p>
