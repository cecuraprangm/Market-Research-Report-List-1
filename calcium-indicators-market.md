<p><p>Calcium indicators are used to measure the concentration of calcium ions in biological systems. There are two main types of calcium indicators: chemical indicators and genetically encoded indicators. Chemical indicators are synthetic molecules that change their fluorescence properties in the presence of calcium ions. Genetically encoded indicators are proteins that are genetically engineered to emit fluorescence when bound to calcium ions. Both types of indicators are widely used in research to study calcium signaling in cells and tissues.</p></p>
