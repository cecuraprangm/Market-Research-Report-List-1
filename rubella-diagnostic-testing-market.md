<p><p>Rubella diagnostic testing refers to the various methods used to detect the presence of rubella virus in individuals. The market for rubella diagnostic testing includes different types of tests such as flow cytometry, latex slide agglutination test, monoclonal antibodies, immunoassays, serological testing, and clinical evaluation. Flow cytometry involves analyzing cells using laser-based technology. Latex slide agglutination test detects the presence of rubella-specific antibodies. Monoclonal antibodies are used to identify rubella antigens. Immunoassays detect rubella-specific antibodies or antigens. Serological testing measures the level of rubella antibodies in the blood. Clinical evaluation involves assessing the symptoms and medical history of patients.</p></p>
