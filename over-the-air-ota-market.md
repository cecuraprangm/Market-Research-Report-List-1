<p><ul><li>Firmware over-the-air (FOTA)</li><li>Software over-the-air (SOTA)</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Over-the-air (OTA) market types include Firmware over-the-air (FOTA) and Software over-the-air (SOTA). FOTA refers to the process of remotely updating firmware on devices without requiring physical connections. This is commonly used for updating the operating system or fixing software bugs. SOTA, on the other hand, involves remotely updating software applications on devices, allowing for new features to be added or existing features to be improved without the need for manual updates. Both FOTA and SOTA provide convenience and efficiency for device manufacturers and users.</p></p>
