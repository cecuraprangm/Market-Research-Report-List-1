<p><p>Self-expanding metal stents (SEMS) are cylindrical mesh-like structures made from a variety of metals such as stainless steel, nitinol, and cobalt-chromium alloys. These stents are primarily used in medical procedures to restore normal blood flow, reinforce weakened vessels, or provide structural support in various anatomical areas of the body. They are particularly useful in treating conditions such as blocked blood vessels, gastrointestinal strictures, and narrowing of the airways.</p><p>The self-expanding metal stents market is expected to exhibit significant growth during the forecast period. This can be attributed to several factors, including increasing prevalence of chronic diseases such as cardiovascular disorders, cancer, and respiratory diseases, which require the use of stents for effective treatment. Additionally, advancements in stent technology, such as the development of bioresorbable stents and drug-eluting stents, are driving market growth.</p><p>Furthermore, the aging population and the subsequent rise in age-related diseases are contributing to the demand for self-expanding metal stents. As the elderly population continues to grow, the incidence of diseases requiring stent placement, such as atherosclerosis and chronic obstructive pulmonary disease, is also increasing.</p><p>In terms of regional analysis, North America currently dominates the self-expanding metal stents market, followed by Europe.
