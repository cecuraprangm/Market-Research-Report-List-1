<p><p>Preventive vaccines are important tools in controlling the spread of infectious diseases. They can be administered through different methods, namely intramuscular injection, nasal spray, and intradermal shot. Intramuscular injection involves injecting the vaccine into the muscle tissue, usually in the upper arm. Nasal spray vaccines are administered through the nose, targeting the respiratory system. Intradermal shots are administered just below the skin surface, using a smaller needle. Each method has its advantages and is chosen based on factors such as the target population, vaccine characteristics, and feasibility of administration.</p></p>
