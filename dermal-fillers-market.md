<p><p>Dermal fillers are cosmetic treatments used to restore volume and smoothen areas of the face, minimizing facial lines and wrinkles. They come in various types, each with unique properties. Hyaluronic acid (HA) fillers, the most common type, hydrate and plump the skin. Calcium hydroxylapatite (CaHA) fillers stimulate collagen production and provide longer-lasting results. Poly-L-lactic acid (PLLA) fillers stimulate collagen gradually over time. Polymethylmethacrylate (PMMA) fillers are synthetic microspheres that remain in the skin as a support structure. Lastly, there are other types of fillers available, each with specific composition and effects. These fillers are used to address different aging concerns and achieve desired aesthetic results.</p></p>
