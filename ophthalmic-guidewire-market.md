<p><p>Ophthalmic guidewires are used in eye surgeries to facilitate the insertion of various instruments and implants. There are two main types of materials used in ophthalmic guidewires: stainless steel and nitinol. Stainless steel guidewires offer high tensile strength and resistance to corrosion, making them suitable for long-term use. Nitinol guidewires, on the other hand, are known for their superelasticity and shape memory, allowing for easy maneuverability during procedures. Both types of guidewires play a crucial role in ensuring the success of ophthalmic surgeries.</p></p>
