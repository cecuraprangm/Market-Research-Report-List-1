<p><p>Self-Expanding Peripheral Vascular Stents are small, metal mesh tubes used to prop open narrowed or blocked blood vessels in the peripheral arteries. <p><p>Self-expanding peripheral vascular stents are medical devices used to treat narrowed or blocked blood vessels in the legs, arms, or other peripheral areas of the body. There are two main types of materials used in these stents - Elgiloy and Nitinol. Elgiloy stents are made from a cobalt-chromium alloy known for its high strength and corrosion resistance. Nitinol stents, on the other hand, are made from a nickel-titanium alloy that is known for its superelasticity and shape memory. Both types of stents are designed to open up blocked blood vessels and improve blood flow.</p></p>
