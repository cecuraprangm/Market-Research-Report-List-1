<p><p>Industrial surfactants are chemical compounds used in various industries for their unique surface-active properties. There are four main types: amphoteric, anionic, cationic, and nonionic surfactants. Amphoteric surfactants possess both positive and negative charges on their molecules, making them compatible with different pH levels. Anionic surfactants carry negative charges and are commonly found in cleaning products due to their ability to remove dirt and oil. Cationic surfactants have positive charges and are often used as disinfectants or fabric softeners. Nonionic surfactants have no charge and are versatile, making them suitable for applications ranging from foaming agents to emulsifiers.</p></p>
