<p><p>The human rabies treatment market includes two main applications: pre-exposure immunization and post-exposure prophylaxis. Pre-exposure immunization involves administering vaccines to individuals who are at high risk of contracting rabies, such as veterinarians and laboratory workers, to prevent infection. On the other hand, post-exposure prophylaxis is the treatment given to individuals who have been exposed to the rabies virus, either through a bite or scratch from an infected animal. This treatment includes a series of vaccines and, in severe cases, the administration of rabies immunoglobulin to prevent the virus from causing illness.</p></p>
