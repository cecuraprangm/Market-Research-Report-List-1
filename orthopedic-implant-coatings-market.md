<p><ul><li>Sintered Bead Coatings</li><li>Plasma Sprayed Metallic Coatings</li><li>Hydroxyapatite (HA) Coatings</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Orthopedic implant coatings refer to the materials used to enhance the performance and longevity of orthopedic implants. There are three main types of coatings in the market. Sintered bead coatings involve the fusion of small beads onto the implant surface, providing a rough texture for bone ingrowth. Plasma sprayed metallic coatings use high-velocity jets to deposit a layer of metal onto the implant, improving its durability. Hydroxyapatite (HA) coatings, made of a calcium phosphate compound, promote bone integration by mimicking its natural composition.</p></p>
