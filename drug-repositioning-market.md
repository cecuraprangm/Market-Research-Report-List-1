<p><p>Drug repositioning, also known as drug repurposing or therapeutic switching, is the process of finding new indications for existing approved drugs that are already on the market. This approach offers several advantages over traditional drug discovery and development, as it leverages existing knowledge about the drug's safety, dosage, and potential side effects, thereby reducing the time, costs, and risks associated with bringing a completely new drug to market.</p><p>The drug repositioning market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 6.1% during the forecast period. The growth of this market can be attributed to several factors. Firstly, the increasing prevalence of chronic diseases such as cancer, neurological disorders, and cardiovascular diseases necessitates the development of new treatment options. Drug repositioning offers a faster and more cost-effective approach to address these unmet medical needs.</p><p>Furthermore, the advancements in technology, such as high-throughput screening and bioinformatics tools, have facilitated the identification of new indications for existing drugs. These tools enable researchers to screen large databases of drugs and identify potential candidates for repositioning based on their molecular targets and mechanisms of action.</p><p>In addition, the growing interest from pharmaceutical companies in drug repositioning is driving market growth. These companies are increasingly recognizing the potential of repurposing existing drugs to expand their product portfolios and extend the patent life of their drugs.</p><p>The latest trends in the drug repositioning market include the use of artificial intelligence and machine learning algorithms to identify drug-disease associations, the development of novel drug combinations through repositioning, and the exploration of repurposing opportunities for rare diseases and orphan drugs.</p><p>Overall, the drug repositioning market is poised for significant growth in the coming years, driven by the need for innovative treatment options, advancements in technology, and the increasing interest from pharmaceutical companies.</p></p>
