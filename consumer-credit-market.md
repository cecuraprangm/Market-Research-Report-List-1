<p><p>Consumer credit refers to the borrowing of funds by individuals to finance their personal expenses, such as purchasing consumer goods, paying for education, or covering medical expenses. Consumer credit plays a significant role in driving economic growth by stimulating consumer spending and boosting demand.</p><p>The global consumer credit market has witnessed substantial growth in recent years and is projected to continue expanding at a strong pace. The market growth is mainly attributed to factors such as rising consumer expenditure, increasing awareness about easy credit options, and technological advancements facilitating quick and convenient lending processes.</p><p>One major trend in the consumer credit market is the growing popularity of online and mobile-based lending platforms. With the increasing penetration of smartphones and internet connectivity, consumers are now able to access loans and credit facilities through online platforms, eliminating the need for physical visits to banks or financial institutions.</p><p>Another trend is the adoption of alternative credit scoring models and the use of big data analytics. Traditional credit scoring methods solely relied on credit history and income details, often excluding individuals with limited credit histories. However, various emerging fintech companies now utilize alternative data points such as social media activities, utility bill payments, and educational background to assess creditworthiness, enabling a larger number of consumers to access credit.</p><p>Furthermore, the market is witnessing the integration of technologies such as artificial intelligence and machine learning to streamline lending processes, reduce approval times, and minimize risk.
