<p><strong>The Medical Device Additive Manufacturing Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Laser Sintering</li><li>Stereolithography</li><li>Electron Beam Melting</li><li>Extrusion</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Medical device additive manufacturing refers to the production of medical devices using advanced techniques such as laser sintering, stereolithography, electron beam melting, and extrusion. Laser sintering involves using lasers to heat and fuse powdered materials layer by layer, while stereolithography uses light to harden liquid resin into solid objects. Electron beam melting utilizes an electron beam to melt and fuse metal powders, and extrusion involves pushing molten material through a nozzle to create 3D structures. These techniques offer precise and customized manufacturing for medical devices.</p></p>
