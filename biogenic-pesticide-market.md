<p><p>Biogenic pesticides consist of three main types: botanical pesticides, which are derived from plants; zooid pesticides, which are derived from animals; and other biogenic pesticides, which may be derived from microbes or other natural sources. Botanical pesticides are commonly used in organic farming and include substances like neem oil and pyrethrum. Zooid pesticides often involve using predators or parasites to control pest populations. Other biogenic pesticides can include substances like bacteria or fungi that naturally suppress pest populations.</p></p>
