<p><ul><li>Inorganic Salt</li><li>Enzyme</li><li>Organic Wax</li><li>Others</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Edible preservatives are commonly categorized into four types: inorganic salt, enzyme, organic wax, and others. Inorganic salt preservatives such as sodium benzoate and potassium sorbate are widely used to prevent microbial growth in food products. Enzyme preservatives work by breaking down organic matter, thus prolonging the shelf life of items like bread and dairy products. Organic wax preservatives create a protective coating around food items, preventing spoilage. Other types of preservatives include antioxidants and antimicrobials, which also help extend the shelf life of various food products.</p></p>
