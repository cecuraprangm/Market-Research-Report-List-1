<p><p>Fire-resistant aluminum composite panels are categorized into four different types based on their components: surface coating, metal skin, core material, and rear skin. Surface coating refers to the protective layer on the panel that enhances its fire-resistant properties. Metal skin is the outer layer of the panel made of aluminum material. The core material is the central layer that adds strength and fire resistance to the panel. Rear skin is the back layer of the panel that provides additional protection and durability.</p></p>
