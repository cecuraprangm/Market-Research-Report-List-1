<p><p>Anti-lock Brake Systems (ABS) for cars are available in different market types based on the number of channels. One-channel ABS operates on one wheel, typically found on older vehicles. Two-channel ABS independently controls brakes on two wheels, improving stability during braking. Three-channel ABS controls brakes on two wheels separately and the other two wheels together, enhancing control in emergency situations. Four-channel ABS individually regulates each wheel, providing the highest level of control and preventing wheels from locking up, ensuring optimal braking performance.</p></p>
