<p><p>Zero-order waveplates are used in optical systems to control the polarization state of light. Zero-order half-wave waveplates produce a 180-degree phase shift in the polarization of light passing through them, while zero-order quarter-wave waveplates produce a 90-degree phase shift. These waveplates are commonly used in applications such as telecommunications, spectroscopy, and imaging. They offer high damage thresholds and low sensitivity to temperature variations, making them ideal for demanding optical systems.</p></p>
