<p><p>The automotive OEM brake friction parts market comprises three main types: brake pads, brake shoes, and other friction components. Brake pads are the most common type, made of a friction material mounted on a metal backing plate and used in disc brake systems. Brake shoes, on the other hand, are used in drum brake systems and consist of a curved metal shoe with a friction lining. The "other" segment includes various small friction components like brake linings, discs, and rotors that contribute to the overall braking system's functionality.</p></p>
