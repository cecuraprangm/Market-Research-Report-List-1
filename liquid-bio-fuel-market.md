<p><p>Liquid biofuel market is primarily divided into two main types: bio-diesel and ethanol. Bio-diesel is a renewable fuel produced from vegetable oils, animal fats, or recycled cooking grease. It is commonly used as a diesel additive or replacement in vehicles. Ethanol, on the other hand, is a biofuel made from fermented sugars or starches, primarily derived from corn or sugarcane. It is commonly blended with gasoline to reduce emissions and improve fuel efficiency. Both bio-diesel and ethanol play a crucial role in the transition towards a more sustainable and environmentally friendly energy sector.</p></p>
