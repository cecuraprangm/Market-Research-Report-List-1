<p><p>A Hardware Security Module (HSM) is a physical device that enhances the security of modern systems. It comes in three different market types: LAN-based, PCle-based, and USB-based. </p><p>LAN-based HSMs are connected to the Local Area Network (LAN), making them accessible to multiple systems across the network. </p><p>PCle-based HSMs are inserted into the computer's PCI Express (PCle) slot and provide dedicated security capabilities directly to the computer. </p><p>USB-based HSMs are plugged into a USB port and are portable, allowing users to easily transfer and use them on various devices. </p><p>All three types offer secure storage of cryptographic keys, encryption/decryption, digital signing, and secure execution of sensitive operations.</p></p>
