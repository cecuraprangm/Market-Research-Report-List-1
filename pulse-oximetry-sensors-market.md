<p><p>Pulse oximetry sensors are medical devices that are used to measure the oxygen saturation levels in a patient's blood. These sensors are typically attached to a patient's finger, earlobe, or toe, and they work by shining a light through the skin and measuring the amount of oxygen that is absorbed by the red blood cells. This information is then displayed on a screen or monitor, allowing healthcare professionals to monitor the patient's oxygen levels in real time.</p><p>The pulse oximetry sensors market has been experiencing significant growth in recent years. The increasing prevalence of respiratory diseases, such as chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD) and asthma, is one of the key factors driving market growth. Additionally, the rising adoption of pulse oximetry sensors in home healthcare settings and the increasing demand for wearable health monitoring devices have also contributed to the market's expansion.</p><p>Moreover, the COVID-19 pandemic has further boosted the demand for pulse oximetry sensors.
