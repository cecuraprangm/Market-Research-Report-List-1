<p><p>Silicone surface additives are categorized into four types based on their charge: anionic, cationic, bisexual, and non-ionic. Anionic additives carry a negative charge and are commonly used as dispersing agents. Cationic additives have a positive charge and are effective as adhesion promoters. Bisexual additives contain both positive and negative charges, offering a dual functionality. Non-ionic additives do not carry a charge and are versatile in their application. Each type of silicone surface additive has distinct properties and is used for specific applications in various industries.</p></p>
